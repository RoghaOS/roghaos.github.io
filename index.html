RoghaOS

Rogha and Eolas, two words from the Irish language.

Rogha meaning choice, freedom. RoghaOS is an operating system for PCs and a firmware for IoT devices, focusing on giving users and administrators freedom to use their OS the way they intend.

Eolas means knowledge, information. We mean access to information and knowledge, from the basic things online to the OSes Heart source code.

RoghaOS was initially imagined being used as a universal Operating System for Education Institutions, being easy to manage and deploy to many devices.

Goal

Our Vision is to provide something you can rely and depend on. Rogha will not change all the time and have a waterfall of frequent versions like other OSes do.

This way you can use your time for things that matter the most, your creations, your control and your management.

It doesn’t matter your profession or area, RoghaOS is made for everyone. For people who simply want to turn their device on and be productive without having to find out where a button had moved to. Giving you the choice to thinker with the OS and change things if you like. With RoghaOS we aim to produce a robust, modular, flexible and stable platform that respects your rights and your choice.

Ideal

The choice to choose and do things the way you want. Or simply the choice of leaving things just the way they are and not changing them.

RoghaOS will not ever go thorough any major change that would affect your workflow. We understand and care about the way you do your work. Make a shell script to automate a task or port a game to RoghaOS and rest assured, it will still work just the way you intended for many years to come.

Design

We are committed to provide a reliable and consistent experience to everyone running RoghaOS.

Learn Once Use Many. Learn and train for the OS once and use it for many times and many years, contrasting some other Operating Systems that have drastically changed their user interface, forcing people and companies to spend money and time adapting to the OS, RoghaOS is committed to a stable GUI design.

No dramatic UI changes. We have only a fixed and static API and guideline, develop for our standard and keep your app alive for ever.

Structure

RoghaOS have to main parts: Eolas the bottom layer, which is where the Kernel, Drivers, APIs and low-level stuff lives, it has no GUI (Graphical User Interface) and it is text based, intended to be used remotely, IoT applications and as well NAS (Network Attached Storage) and Firewall applications.

The GUI is called Rogha, similar to the whole Operating System but without the “OS” part.

This approach is done in many other Operating Systems and facilitates the development and maintenance of the platform, some examples of other Operating Systems that have been following this approach are: OpenIndiana, forked from OpenSolaris, built on top of the illumos project. macOS, the whole OS is named macOS but the under the hood portion of it is called Darwin, which is a Unix-based BSD. Windows, originally Windows was just a layer siting on top of MS-DOS.
